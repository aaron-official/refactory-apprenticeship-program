# Design Patterns — Analogies

---

## 1. Singleton (Creational)

Think of the **headmaster of a school**. There is only one headmaster at any time. No matter which teacher or student needs to speak to "the headmaster," they all go to the same one person. You can't create a second headmaster — there's only ever one, and everyone accesses that same one.

---

## 2. Strategy (Behavioral)

Think of a **boda rider deciding which route to take** from Wandegeya to Ntinda. Depending on traffic, they might take Bombo Road, Gayaza Road, or a back route through Kamwokya. The destination is the same, the rider is the same — only the route (strategy) changes depending on the situation.

---

## 3. Observer (Behavioral)

Think of **subscribing to an MTN mobile money account alert**. Whenever money enters or leaves the account (the subject), all registered contacts — SMS, email, app notification — automatically receive an alert. They don't have to keep checking the account; they get notified the moment something changes.

---

## 4. Decorator (Structural)

Think of a **boda rider starting with a basic bike**. Then they add a rain cover. Then a delivery bag. Then a safety vest. The original bike never changed — each addition was just wrapped on top of it. You can keep adding or removing extras without rebuilding the bike from scratch.

---

## 5. Proxy (Structural)

Think of a **receptionist at an office**. You don't walk straight into the manager's office — the receptionist first checks who you are, whether you have an appointment, and whether your request is appropriate. Only then do they let you through. The receptionist has the same role as the manager (handling visitors) but controls access to the real person.

---

## 6. Façade (Structural)

Think of **ordering food on a delivery app**. You just tap a few buttons and food arrives. Behind the scenes, the app is coordinating the restaurant, the payment system, the delivery rider, and the GPS tracking — but you see none of that. The app is the façade: one simple interface hiding a whole system of complex operations.

---

## 7. Bridge (Structural)

Think of a **news anchor delivering the news**. The type of story (breaking news, sports, weather) and the channel it's delivered on (TV, radio, social media) are kept completely separate. You can have any type of story delivered on any channel, and adding a new channel doesn't affect the types of stories — and vice versa. The two sides evolve independently.
